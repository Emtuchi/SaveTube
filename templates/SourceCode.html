To determine the permissions related to downloading source code from a website, you typically need to refer to the website's terms of service, terms and conditions, or any licensing information provided by the website owner.

Here are a few methods you can use:

1. Website's Terms of Service (ToS): Check the website's official Terms of Service or Terms and Conditions. This documentation often includes information about the permitted use of the website's content, including whether downloading source code is allowed and under what conditions.

2. Robots.txt File: Websites may specify crawling permissions and restrictions in their robots.txt file, which is a text file that communicates with web crawlers and specifies which parts of the site should not be accessed by them. However, note that robots.txt files mainly focus on search engine crawlers rather than human users.

3. Licensing Information: Some websites might have specific licensing information for their content or source code. They might use licenses like GPL, MIT, Apache, etc., which outline the permissions and limitations for using, modifying, and distributing their source code.

4. Contacting Website Owner/Administrator: If you're unsure about the permissions or licensing for downloading source code from a particular website, it might be best to directly contact the website owner or administrator to seek permission or clarification.

5. Legal Advice: For complex or uncertain cases, consulting a legal professional might be necessary to ensure compliance with copyright laws and terms of use.

Remember, respecting the intellectual property rights and permissions of website content is crucial. Always ensure that you have the necessary permissions before downloading or using source code from any website.
